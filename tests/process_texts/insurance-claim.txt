When a customer files an insurance claim, the intake officer logs it and reads off the claim amount. Claims under €500 are settled automatically; anything higher goes to full assessment. In a full assessment an adjuster inspects the reported damage and, at the same time, the fraud unit runs a background check, and the case advances only once both are done. Before deciding, the adjuster may request one or more supporting documents — a police report, medical records, or repair estimates — and proceeds as soon as just the requested ones arrive. The claim is then either approved for payout or declined. A customer who disputes a declined claim has it reopened and reassessed.
